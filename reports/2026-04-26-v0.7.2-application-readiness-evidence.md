# v0.7.2 Application Readiness Evidence

This report closes KIU-748 through KIU-752 for the Application Readiness Model pass. It is internal deterministic evaluation evidence, not external blind review, real-user validation, domain-expert validation, or same-source cangjie victory evidence.

## What Changed

v0.7.2 adds a general application-readiness layer instead of editing generated sample artifacts directly.

- `readiness.py` defines shared readiness statuses, findings, and aggregation.
- `coverage_model.py` diagnoses source capability coverage without enforcing a fixed skill count.
- `mechanism_evidence.py` scores whether evidence can act as primary mechanism proof.
- `transfer_fit.py` adds mechanism, incentive, authority, time-horizon, disconfirming-evidence, and anti-condition questions for transfer candidates.
- `world_alignment.py` now exposes `use_state` and `transfer_fit` in application gates while preserving original source skills.
- `render.py` writes `coverage-readiness.json` during generated-run rendering and uses rendered artifact text as coverage evidence when ids are abstract.

The implementation follows the project rule: sample-driven diagnosis, model-driven repair, orchestration-driven landing, and rules only as guardrails.

## Readiness Interpretation

The application-readiness pass improves the evidence structure without claiming that every upstream extraction weakness is solved.

- Transfer-fit is working as a general application gate: transfer-like outputs now surface `ask_more_context` transfer readiness questions instead of pretending historical or principle transfer is directly ready.
- Low-risk non-dilution remains protected: Poor Charlie includes one `not_applicable` transfer-fit gate and does not force every mental model into operational transfer questioning.
- Effective Requirements improved from the earlier thin one-skill shape to three published artifacts while keeping proxy and practical scores above 94.
- Financial Statement passes final-artifact proxy/practical gates, but the raw-book run still emits several exercise or section-style artifact ids; this is a generation-quality caveat, not a reason to hide the result.
- Shiji and Mao still show coverage warnings because current graph communities are chapter-like source clusters while generated skills are cross-chapter judgment abstractions. This is a real diagnostic limitation of the coverage model and upstream extraction, not a release-ready claim of complete source coverage.

## Remaining Caveats

- Coverage readiness is diagnostic, not a release blocker in this pass. It correctly keeps warnings visible, but Shiji/Mao prove the model still needs a future bridge from chapter/community coverage to cross-chapter conceptual coverage.
- Financial Statement Source `87.9` is below the preferred 90+ band. The final usage/practical layer is strong, but raw-book source extraction still needs future cleanup for exercise/section-like candidates.
- The evidence does not prove external blind preference, real-user value, or same-source cangjie superiority.

## Closure Decision

KIU-748 through KIU-752 are internally complete for v0.7.2.

This pass can claim:

- application-readiness schema, coverage diagnostics, mechanism evidence guardrails, and transfer-fit gates are implemented;
- five diagnostic samples remain above the v0.7.2 internal proxy/practical thresholds;
- source pollution remains zero;
- source-only mode and world-layer isolation are preserved.

This pass must not claim:

- full source coverage for broad raw-book materials;
- external blind closure;
- cangjie same-source victory;
- domain-expert validation.
