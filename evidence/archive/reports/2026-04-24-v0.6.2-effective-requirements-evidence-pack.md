# KiU v0.6.2 Effective Requirements Evidence Pack

Date: 2026-04-24

## Version Goal

v0.6.2 closes the Effective Requirements branch regression found after v0.6.1: a workflow-heavy book must still be usable through an installable gateway, but quality gains must not come from collapsing deterministic workflow logic into thick skills.

This work follows the `AGENTS.md` workflow-vs-agentic boundary rule: judge `workflow certainty` and `context certainty` separately; keep high/high deterministic procedures in `workflow_candidates/`; promote only judgment-rich interpretation, conflict arbitration, or context assembly into `bundle/skills/`.

## Implemented Tickets

- `KIU-669`: expanded `workflow-gateway` usage evidence from one happy-path smoke review to six routing/boundary cases: primary routing, routing by hint, sequencing, missing-context clarification, agentic-refusal, and do-not-inline.
- `KIU-670`: added two Effective Requirements agentic skills only for judgment-heavy cases: `solution-to-problem-reframing` and `stakeholder-resistance-tradeoff`.
- `KIU-671`: added workflow-gateway anti-drift preflight and review reporting. A gateway fails preflight if its routes do not match `workflow_candidates/` or if `SKILL.md` inlines deterministic checklist steps.

## Interpretation

The Effective Requirements source remains workflow-heavy. v0.6.2 does not treat a low thick-skill count as failure by itself. The improved shape is mixed:

- Deterministic procedure remains in `workflow_candidates/`.
- `workflow-gateway` is a thin callable router and not a domain-thick skill.
- Only two judgment-rich themes are promoted: solution-to-problem reframing and stakeholder resistance tradeoff.

This is aligned with KiU's In Use goal: generated output is installable and usable while preserving auditability and workflow/agentic separation.
